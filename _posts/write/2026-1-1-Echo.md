* 从算法改进得到的启示：
    * 比起优化目的地（概率密度最大），优化每一步的方向（梯度，或者说score）相对容易一些，免去了normalizing constant（难计算）。对应到人生选择，就是相比寻找宏远目标（目的地），判断当下的选择对我们的影响（梯度/score）更重要。
    * Perturb p(x)  with Gaussian noise的过程和画画里的高斯模糊好像是一个逻辑，对p(x)里的每个样本点x施加一个高斯模糊效果，整张图的所有位置叠加起来就是最终效果。只不过是复制了很多图层，每个图层高斯模糊的力度不同。perturb的目的是利用这些噪声样本去探索稀疏区域的梯度（score）。对应到日常：不要追求完美/optimal，去创造粗糙甚至拙劣的作品（imperfection），作为探针得到下一步优化方向。

* 📖：
  * 《Color&Light》：
    * 传统绘画降低明度时加黑色反倒影响色相，是因为黑色颜料和理想的黑色还是有距离，加入互补色反倒更接近正确颜色。

# 碎碎念

尝试了用AI帮我从上面的流水帐总结这一年的发现，内容虽然有一定逻辑，但似乎少了点“人味儿”——说不清具体是什么感觉。而且不同的AI风味也不同，比如MiniMax有一股“老中味儿”，ChatGPT像老实本分的管家，Gemini则相对跳脱一点。

今年的主题为什么叫Echo呢？似乎还是和一些小爱好有关——二胡和笛子都是很早埋下的想法，中间有了点行动但一直没深入。今年都有了比较明显的进展：可以吹、拉一点喜欢的曲子了。虽然依旧磕磕绊绊，但是那种感觉好神奇——原本以为遥不可及的事情，在真正踏出那一步之后，才发现：“诶？好像真的可以做到诶。”还有去公园在众目睽睽之下写生也是长久以来的想法，但心理上一直没迈出那一步，现在迈出了发现水彩真的好难呀，可能比承受路人的轮番目光检阅还要难。

好吧，其实也不完全是因为这些小爱好，一个很大的原因是2025年是写年终总结的第10年啦，从最初短小的记录，到如今浩浩荡荡的流水账，总结的体量也在一年年膨胀，如同一棵小树苗的年轮一圈圈荡漾开。于是，2025就成了呼应2016年Reply的Echo。可以看到16年也在折腾水彩——那时大概还没意识到这是个多深的坑；那时候买的本子到现在也只开了个头……除了那时的古琴，现在又开辟了新大陆，同时探索弦乐管乐弹拨乐。影音书籍依然是最长情的伙伴，不过现在会记录更多想法了。那个当年帮老师改卷子的人，现在要改自己的卷子了。当然，花钱也愈发“猖狂”了……

以上种种，如同十年前发出的疑问，在十年后的今日震荡出的遥远回声。弹指十年间，相似的故事反复上演，仿佛延时摄影般：故事的主角留在原地，但周遭却瞬息万变、沧海桑田。直到鬓角冒出的老年斑给一切盖戳，才终于接收到岁月的信号——我并不在原地。我只是像月相般，在夜空里反复定格，但月亮还是那个月亮。

I tried using AI to help distill this year’s rather stream-of-consciousness summary into something more structured. While the result had a certain internal logic, it seemed to be missing a bit of “human warmth”—it’s hard to say exactly what, but you can feel it. Different AIs also have very different flavors: MiniMax carries a distinctly old-school Chinese tone, ChatGPT feels like an earnest, well-behaved housekeeper, and Gemini is comparatively more playful and jumpy.

So why is this year’s theme called Echo? It seems to trace back to some long-held personal interests. The erhu and the bamboo flute were ideas planted a long time ago—there were small attempts along the way, but I never really went deep. This year, though, there was visible progress: I can now play a few pieces I genuinely like. It’s still clumsy, of course, but the feeling is strangely magical. Things that once felt completely out of reach, once you actually take that first step, suddenly turn into: “Huh… maybe this really is doable.”
The same goes for sketching outdoors in public parks—another long-standing idea that I never quite dared to act on. Now that I finally have, I’ve discovered that watercolor painting is genuinely hard—perhaps even harder than enduring the steady gaze of passing strangers.

Well, it’s not just about these small hobbies. A much bigger reason is that 2025 marks the tenth year of writing an annual reflection. What started as short, modest notes has gradually expanded into these sprawling, meandering chronicles. The summaries themselves have grown year by year, like the rings of a young tree spreading outward. And so 2025 becomes an Echo of 2016’s Reply.
Looking back, you can see that in 2016 I was already dabbling in watercolor—probably without realizing how deep that rabbit hole was. The sketchbook I bought back then is still barely begun. Aside from the guqin I played at the time, I’ve since opened up new territory, exploring string, wind, and plucked instruments. Films, music, and books remain my most loyal companions, though now I tend to write down more of my thoughts. The person who once helped teachers grade exam papers is now grading her own. And, unsurprisingly, my spending habits have become increasingly… "unrestrained".

All of this feels like distant echoes of questions posed ten years ago, reverberating into the present. In the blink of an eye, a decade has passed. Similar stories replay themselves again and again, like a time-lapse sequence: the protagonist seems to stay in place, while everything around them shifts at breathtaking speed, seas turning into land and back again. It’s only when age spots appear at the temples, stamping everything with undeniable proof, that the signal of time finally gets through—I was never standing still.
I was simply, like the phases of the moon, repeatedly frozen in the night sky. But the moon is still the moon.
